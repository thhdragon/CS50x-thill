2) Hypothesize about why the way you’ve calculated this aura might not be very representative of the
listener.
What better ways of calculating this aura would you propose?

A better way of calculating this aura could be done if we had access to the play counts of each
song. Instead of calculating a flat average it might yield a more accurate aura if the calculations
were done using a weighted average formula and the play counts. One big problem I see with taking
the average of your top 100 songs is that without knowing the listening frequency, song #99 could
have 1000 plays on it and song #100 could have 50 but they would both have the same weight in a flat
average calculation. For accuracy it would be important to differentiate whether or not the user actually
let the song play and didn't immediately skip it while in shuffle or radio mode. Perhaps a metric
such as the user listened to the song for at least N seconds before skipping. Also a lot could be
learned from paying attention to what the user does immediately skip, if there is a correlation
between the attributes of songs frequently skipped it could be used to give those types of songs
lower weight in the aura algorithm.
